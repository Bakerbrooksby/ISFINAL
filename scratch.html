The College Football Playoff

The College Football Playoff (CFP) is the postseason system used to determine the national champion in NCAA Division I FBS football. Instead of relying solely on polls or computer rankings, the CFP uses a selection committee that evaluates teams based on performance, strength of schedule, head-to-head results, and other factors. The top teams are chosen to compete in a bracket-style playoff, with semifinal games hosted by major bowl sites and a final played at a neutral location. Designed to create a more competitive and fair path to the championship, the CFP has become one of the most anticipated events in American sports, drawing massive viewership and intense debate each season.

My Favorite to Win

The Oregon Ducks come into the CFP with one of the most balanced and potent teams in the country, making them serious contenders for the national title. This season they’ve compiled an 11–1 record, averaging 38.2 points per game while allowing just 14.8 — a combination of high-powered offense and disciplined defense. Their offense — led by quarterback Dante Moore — moves efficiently, and the team consistently generates big plays, keeping opposing defenses off-balance. Meanwhile, their schedule strength and quality wins have boosted their résumé enough to earn a top seed and favorable path through the bracket. Moreover, they’ll play key games at home — a formidable advantage — which could tip tight matchups in their favor.

Why You Should Route For Oregon

A football fan should root for Oregon because the Ducks embody everything exciting about the modern college game: speed, creativity, and electric playmaking. Few programs bring as much energy to the field, with an offense that attacks aggressively and a defense built on intensity and athleticism. Oregon also has one of the most passionate fan bases and unique traditions in college football—from the iconic uniforms to the electric atmosphere at Autzen Stadium. On top of that, the program has a reputation for player development, strong coaching, and a culture centered on hard work and resilience. Rooting for Oregon means cheering for a team that’s bold, entertaining, and always pushing the game forward.
